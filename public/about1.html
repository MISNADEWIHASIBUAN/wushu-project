ABOUT IWUF

The International Wushu Federation (IWUF), established on October 3rd, 1990, is the international federation (IF) which governs wushu in all its forms worldwide. Currently, the IWUF has 156 members, across 5 continental federations. The IWUF is recognized by the International Olympic Committee (IOC), and is a member of ARISF Association of IOC Recognised International Sports Federations) , GAISF (Global Association of International Sports Federations) and IWGA (International World Games Association).

VISION

Making wushu an Olympic sport and a healthy way of life for people around the globe.

MISSION

The IWUF develops wushu throughout the world, enriching people’s lives both physically and mentally and promoting friendship and understanding , free from all forms of discrimination, between all people worldwide through the spirit of sport.

IWUF CHRONOLOGY

  • 1990 Founded on October 3rd during the Asian Games.
  • 1991 The 1st World Wushu Championships was held in Beijing, China.
  • 1994 Accepted as a member of the GAISF during its 28th congress.
  • 2001 IWUF signs WADA agreement.
  • 2002 IOC officially recognizes the IWUF during its 113th session held in Lake City – Utah, United States of America.
  • 2002 The 1st Sanda World Cup is held in Shanghai, China.
  • 2004 The 1st World Traditional Wushu Festival, known today as World Kungfu Championships, a sport for all event, is held in Zhengzhou, China.
  • 2006 The 1st World Junior Wushu Championships are held in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.
  • 2008 Beijing 2008 Wushu Tournament is held in conjunction of the 2008 Summer Olympic Games; IOC President, Jacques Rogge awards first medal.
  • 2011 Wushu shortlisted for inclusion in the Tokyo 2020 Summer Olympic Games.
  • 2012 IWUF headquarters are established in Lausanne, Switzerland.
  • 2014 Nanjing 2014 Youth Wushu Tournament is held as part of the IOC’s culture and education program. Wushu is one of four sports featured in the IOC’s experimental Sports Lab held concurrently with the Nanjing Youth Olympic Games (YOG.)
  • 2014 The 1st World Taijiquan Championships are held in Chengdu, China.
  • 2015 IWUF signs cooperation convention with FISU.
  • 2015 Wushu is shortlisted with 7 other sports for recommendation as an additional sport for the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games.
  • 2016 1st Taolu World Cup is held in Fuzhou, China.
  • 2017 Wushu Competition debuts in the 29th Summer Universiade.
  • 2018 Wushu Competition debuts in World University Games (FISU) held in Macau, China.
  • 2020 Wushu is included as an official sport in Dakar 2022 Youth Olympic Games.
  • 2021 IWUF is accepted as an official member of the IWGA.

IWUF AROUND THE WORLD

The IWUF’s group of official world championships, held biennially, include: The World Wushu Championships, the World Junior Wushu Championships, World Kungfu Championships, World Sanda Cup, World Taijiquan Championships, and Taolu World Cup.

Wushu is currently included as a competition or exhibition sport in the Asian Games, The World Games, World Combat Games, Islamic Solidarity Games, African Youth Games, Universiade (FISU), and World University Games (FISU).

IWUF’s Anti-Doping Rules are adopted and implemented in accordance with the federation’s responsibilities under the World Anti-Doping Code of WADA, and in furtherance of IWUF's continuing efforts to eradicate doping in sport.
